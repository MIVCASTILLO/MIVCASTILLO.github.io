What is Being Done to Combat Hate Crimes?

 

Cases revolving around hate crimes are investigated mainly by law enforcement in the state and local levels. In some instances, law enforcement collaborates with the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) to help solve cases. The FBI is responsible for investigating crimes that violate federal civil rights statutes, so in crimes where civil rights have been violated, the FBI works with law enforcement on all levels. Another way in which the FBI fights against hate crimes is by lending law enforcement the resources that could help speed up the process of investigation. According to fbi.gov, some of their resources include “forensic expertise, experience in identification and proof of hate-based motivations.” Public outreach, as well as training, are two other ways that the FBI can assist law enforcement during hate crime investigations.
